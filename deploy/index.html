● Safety, by detection

The conflicts you can't afford
to find out about after.

Every signal feeding the engine is checked against the full forward picture. Risks are surfaced at the point they emerge, not at the point they become incidents.

RISK CLASSES
Five categories of safety event, continually re-evaluated as inputs shift. Each carries a window, a cascade, and a recommendation.
UKC
Under-keel clearance violation.

Live draft, tide curve and channel survey data scored against vessel commitment point. Flagged before the channel becomes the wrong place to be.

AIR DRAUGHT
Bridge & overhead clearance.

Vessel air draught checked against tide-adjusted bridge clearance for every fixed structure on the inbound and outbound track. Margin held visible.

BERTH OVERLAP
Berth & swing-room conflicts.

Berth occupancy, swing-room interference and adjacent-berth crane reach checked across every projected movement. Contention surfaced at variance, not at arrival.

PILOT GAP
Pilot coverage gaps.

Roster vs. demand, cross-checked against transit time, hours-of-rest and exchange points. Pilot shortfall flagged before the first vessel commits inbound.

SERVICE CLASH
Tug & line double-bookings.

Tug capacity, bollard pull suitability and line-gang availability validated against every concurrent movement in the window. No two vessels assigned the same asset.

PRINCIPLE
Every safety event detected is recorded, surfaced or not, acted on or not. The ledger is the audit trail.
