A signal lands.

No alarm. No alert fatigue. Horizon takes the input and runs it forward.

STEP 02 · 09:43 UTC

The cascade is modelled.

A 0.6kn variance, taken alone, is nothing. Taken with the tide curve, the next inbound, and the pilot roster, it's a 28-minute decision window with two further movements at risk.

Horizon evaluates against every projected movement in the next four hours. The conflict is ranked by cascade impact, not arrival order.

STEP 03 · 09:44 UTC

Options, with trade-offs.

Three viable plays. Each carries the alternatives evaluated, the cascade impact, and the window in which the call must be made. The operator decides, Horizon shows the cost of each path.

STEP 04 · 10:11 UTC

The call is made.

The operator selects Option A. The decision flows downstream, pilot reassignment, tug tasking, line gang scheduling, Korowai's revised ETD, without a single phone call.

Coordination state is the platform. The radio is for what it should be for.

STEP 05 · 13:24 UTC

The ledger holds.

Talara is alongside. Korowai is on her revised window. Every input that informed the call, every alternative considered, every downstream task, captured. Audit-ready by default.

The decision ledger isn't a separate report. It's the side-effect of doing the job.
